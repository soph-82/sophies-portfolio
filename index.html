Dreamy Bites ||
Customer and Product Analysis using Power BI

The objective of the Dreamy Bites Product & Customer Analysis Dashboard was to transform raw sales, expense, and customer data from September 2019 to December 2020 into a clear and actionable business intelligence report. The dashboard was designed to help the business understand product profitability, customer contribution, sales trends, and operational performance while offering a user-friendly interface for filtering and decision-making. Key metrics such as total profit ($2.72M), total expense ($1.97M), total quantity sold (1M units), and profit margin (58%) were highlighted to give management a complete view of business health across both products and customers. This project successfully delivered a two-page interactive Power BI report that identifies top-performing products (e.g., Chocolate Chip) and high-value customers (e.g., ACME Bites), while also exposing underperforming segments and seasonal peaks. Using Excel, Power Query, Power BI, and DAX, I built profit–expense comparisons, sales trend analysis, order distribution views, and target gauges that support fast strategic decision-making. Based on the insights, I recommend scaling production for consistently high-demand products, optimizing costs for items with lower margins, strengthening relationships with top customers, and launching targeted promotions for low-performing customer accounts or product lines.

Tri-fleet Industry||
Sales Analysis using Power BI service

In this project for Tri-Fleet Industries, the objective was to transform complex, multi-dimensional sales data into secure, role-based insights that stakeholders could easily explore and present. The organization needed visibility into overall sales performance against targets while also enabling managers to analyze product, regional, and territory-level performance without exposing data outside their scope. Static reporting limited flexibility during reviews and made it difficult to support different audiences from a single analytics solution. To address this, I designed and deployed an interactive Power BI dashboard using Power BI Service, incorporating scorecards, product-level analysis, and time-based trend insights. Row-Level Security (RLS) was implemented to ensure managers accessed only their respective performance views, while executives received a consolidated, high-level perspective. The solution centralized visibility into $10M+ in sales and 99K+ units sold, reduced reporting effort by approximately 60% through automation and self-service analytics, and enabled insight-driven recommendations around seasonality, product performance, and operational focus .

Clarity Clear Suicide
Analysis using Power BI

This Power BI project, Clarity Clear Suicide Analytics, was developed to analyze global suicide trends across 101 countries, focusing on key demographic dimensions such as age group, gender, and generational cohorts. Using Power Query for data transformation and interactive visuals, the dashboard highlights critical insights including the high prevalence of suicides among males, the significantly affected 35–54 and 55–74 age groups, and the generational impact—where Boomers, the Silent Generation, and Gen X record the highest numbers. Advanced Power BI features such as Key Influencers and AI-driven segmentation were used to uncover hidden patterns, helping to identify conditions under which suicide rates are more or less likely to occur. Based on the findings, several recommendations were derived to guide targeted mental-health interventions. These include prioritizing support for middle-aged and older adults, expanding men-focused mental-health awareness, and strengthening national reporting systems for more reliable data. Interventions such as workplace mental-health programs, elderly support networks, and stigma-reduction initiatives are essential to addressing the identified risk groups. Overall, the project demonstrates the power of data analytics in supporting informed decisions on a sensitive global issue and showcases strong analytical, visualization, and insight-generation skills using Power BI.

NEXTGEM EMPLOYEE SUCCESS ANALYSIS
using SQL

Next Gen Corp is a rapidly expanding tech and marketing company focused on delivering digital innovation solutions across multiple departments, including Engineering, HR, Sales, and Marketing. As the organization grew, leadership struggled to track workforce performance, salary distribution, hiring trends, and turnover rates effectively. Using SQL to clean, transform, and analyze employee data, I developed a comprehensive Power BI dashboard that centralizes all major HR insights. The solution highlights key metrics such as a $5M total salary expense, $81K average salary, 4.14 average performance score, and detailed department comparisons. It also uncovers hiring patterns, job-title salary structures, and turnover distribution—showing that 57% of exits occurred in 2024, a critical insight for HR strategy. The dashboard provides a clear, data-driven view of employee success, enabling leadership to identify high-performing departments like Engineering and Sales while pinpointing areas needing support, such as HR and departments with lower performance scores. Based on these insights, I recommend conducting deeper analysis into the causes of 2024 turnover, implementing retention initiatives for departments with lower satisfaction, and aligning recruitment efforts with workforce demand patterns identified through SQL-based hiring analysis. Additionally, optimizing salary structures across job titles can improve fairness and reduce internal discrepancies. Together, these actions will strengthen employee engagement, improve performance, and support long-term organizational growth.

PURESIP SALES PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS USING POWER BI

Next Gen Corp is a mid-sized technology and marketing firm focused on innovation, digital transformation, and customer-centric solutions. As the company expanded its workforce across Engineering, Marketing, Sales, and HR, leadership struggled to monitor salary expenses, employee performance trends, department turnover, and hiring activity. To provide clarity and improve decision-making, I developed a comprehensive Power BI dashboard that centralizes employee success metrics and highlights key workforce insights. The dashboard reveals that Next Gen Corp maintains an annual salary expense of $5M, with an average departmental salary of $81K and an average performance score of 4.14 across all teams. Engineering and Sales lead in performance, while Marketing holds the highest total performance contribution (570). The company recorded 26 employees earning above $80K, and hiring trends show consistent recruitment across departments. Turnover analysis shows that 2024 carries 57% of total exits, a critical insight for HR planning. Overall, the dashboard achieves full visibility into employee performance, salary distribution, turnover patterns, departmental comparisons, and job-title salary benchmarks—empowering leadership with data-driven workforce insights. To strengthen organizational success, I recommend deeper root-cause analysis behind higher turnover in 2024, enhanced retention programs for HR and lower-performing teams, and salary optimization based on job roles to reduce discrepancies. Additionally, the company could leverage hiring pattern insights to align recruitment with periods of strong business demand. Implementing these recommendations will help Next Gen Corp enhance employee satisfaction, reduce turnover, and optimize workforce performance for long-term growth.
